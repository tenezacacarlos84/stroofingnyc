Signs You Need Waterproofing

  • Water Stains - Brown or yellow stains on walls or floors indicate water intrusion
  • Mold or Mildew - Musty odors or visible mold, especially in basements
  • Foundation Cracks - Cracks in foundation walls or floors allow water to enter
  • Efflorescence - White powdery residue on walls indicates water evaporation
  • Bowing Walls - Walls that bow or bulge indicate water pressure buildup
  • High Humidity - Excessive humidity throughout your home

Frequently Asked Questions

Waterproofing costs vary based on the area to be treated, the method used, and the severity of the problem. We provide free estimates with detailed pricing so you know exactly what to expect.

Interior waterproofing addresses water already inside your basement (French drains, sump pumps, sealants). Exterior waterproofing prevents water from entering in the first place (exterior membranes, drainage systems). Often, a combination provides the best protection.

Quality waterproofing systems can last 10-20+ years or more with proper maintenance. We'll explain the expected lifespan of your specific solution and provide maintenance tips.

Having a wet basement can significantly impact your home's value and saleability. Many buyers request waterproofing as a contingency. Investing in waterproofing before selling can actually increase your home's value and attract more buyers.
